# Common Questions Taxonomy

Use this taxonomy to classify repeated questions in a single transcript or across multiple transcripts. Always include the likely underlying intent and a recommended prep answer.

## Categories

| Category | Client question pattern | Likely underlying intent | Prep-answer focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Price / Budget | Cost, fee, estimate, discount, payment, cheaper alternative | Budget fit, negotiation anchor, fear of surprise costs | Assumptions, range policy, what changes price, next estimating step |
| Timeline | How long, start date, delivery duration, review duration, implementation duration | Urgency, planning, internal deadlines, opportunity cost | Phase breakdown, dependencies, what the team controls vs does not control |
| Approval / Compliance | Regulator comments, legal review, approval, eligibility, policy, code, compliance | Fear of rejection, delay, liability, or professional exposure | Process ownership, evidence quality, uncertainty boundaries |
| Technical Feasibility | Does it work, integration, system limits, edge cases, data validity | Tests credibility and implementation fit | Scope, assumptions, proof points, known limitations |
| Delivery / Implementation | Who does what, handoff, onboarding, project execution, support | Execution risk and accountability | Ownership map, delivery model, communication cadence |
| Risk / Liability | Guarantees, responsibility, errors, what if it fails | Trust and downside protection | Avoid unsupported guarantees; explain controls, review, escalation |
| Scope / Customization | Special workflow, custom fields, unusual project, tailored output | Flexibility and feasibility | Feasibility check, cost/timeline impact, approval path |
| Competitor / Alternative | Other vendors, cheaper quote, manual workflow, generic AI tools | Comparison shopping or negotiation | Decision criteria and verified differentiators |
| Internal Approval | Partner review, executive sign-off, legal review, budget approval | Internal decision path | Ask who else must approve and what evidence they need |
| Editable / Client-Facing Deliverable | Word, editable PDF, branding, selectable sections, hide pricing | Need to make outputs client-facing and company-specific | Current export options, roadmap, editable fields, branding workflow |
| Data Freshness / Source Validity | How current, source conflict, policy update, data mismatch | Trust, liability, and source-risk testing | Source type, confidence flag, human verification, revision process |
| Trial / Pilot | Free trial, sandbox, demo, prototype, test project | Adoption risk and proof before buying | Paid pilot, demo environment, success criteria, scope boundaries |
| Generic AI / Manual Workflow Comparison | AI tools, spreadsheets, internal process, internet research | Wants differentiation from existing tools | Compare workflow, sources, structured output, assumptions, and review |
| Lead Generation / Pipeline Fit | Find prospects, identify opportunities, screen lists, qualify leads | Wants pipeline leverage, not only analysis | Criteria, geography, data sources, confidence, output schema, custom pricing |

## Cross-Call Pattern Rules

When analyzing multiple transcripts:

1. Count recurring themes, not just exact wording.
2. Group questions by underlying intent.
3. Highlight topics where the team's answers repeatedly receive `Watch`, `Risk`, or `Missing`.
4. Build a preparation library from the highest-frequency and highest-risk themes.
5. Note whether a question appears in a specific client type, such as strict expert clients, quiet executives, price-driven buyers, legal reviewers, technical evaluators, or founder-led teams.

## Prep Answer Requirements

A recommended prep answer should include:

- The direct answer.
- The assumption or boundary.
- The proof point or process step.
- The follow-up action.
- A phrase to avoid if the topic is risky.
